Background

Unlike many of my esteemed colleagues in the Seattle Bicycle Club, I am relatively new to biking. Usually I'm out hiking in the mountains or running along the Burke Gilman trail. But at the wee tender age of 30 I bought my first 'adult' bicycle for two reasons: (1) I wanted to do a local multi-sport adventure race, which included a mountain biking leg, and (2) I wanted to do the STP. The only problem was that I'd only ridden on a bicycle perhaps 3 or 4 times since childhood. So I bought an inexpensive Cannondale hybrid and started training. Enter the Seattle Bicycle Club -- a great place for someone who is new to cycling to learn the ropes, go on fun rides, and meet terrific people at the same time. I've accomplished both of my original biking goals, but somewhere in the process I found I really love this new sport, so I plan to continue riding with and supporting the SBC for a long time to come.

Club Volunteering

Shortly after joining SBC, the call came out for a new newsletter editor. I'd actually contemplated editing as a career choice at one time, despite never having officially edited anything in my life. So I couldn't resist the temptation to volunteer for this position, and I've enjoyed it even more than I would have imagined. I'm always looking for new material for the newsletter, so send your ideas to articles @ seattlebicycle.com!
