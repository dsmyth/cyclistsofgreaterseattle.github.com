Stephanie Roche

Background

Back in the mid-eighties the lightweight helmet was born so I decided I would take up cycling. Immediately after that decision, I was further motivated by the prospect of touring the Ice fields Parkway between Banff and Jasper. I became a non-stop hamster on the Burke-Gilman trail and Queen Anne became my training hill. I figured that if I could climb Queen Anne four times non-stop (a gain of 500 feet each time) that I would be ready for the mountain passes in the Canadian Rockies. Amazingly, it worked. Since then I’ve stayed local with my rides, completing STP four times--once in one day. It’s been a few years since I’ve cycled regularly. One misty morning a few months back, I decided to give it a try again. I accidentally hooked up with an SBC ride to Edmonds. I had a good time and it felt great to be on my bike. I’m looking forward to regular cycling, this time with a focus on SBC weekend tours. But I’ve made a hotel reservation for STP as well!

Club Volunteering

After joining, I came regularly to the monthly potlucks and enjoyed meeting other members. The speakers were excellent and I always had a good time. Shortly, someone nominated me for President and here I am. I’ve never been involved in a club in this manner and I’m glad it’s SBC. The people are enthusiastic, smart, and very supportive. I think it’s a great club and hope to have lots of people join in the fun.
