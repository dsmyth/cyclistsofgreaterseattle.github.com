Mark Peterson

Background

"My first bike was a single speed in second grade. Back then I used it to commute to school even got busted for riding it on school grounds. I still do not agree with the conviction. The arresting teacher had a loose interpretation of the rules in my opinion. Next was a three speed for a junior high paper route. That bike lasted through high school."

"I didn't ride in college; walking fit the bill for the most part. My current bike is a reasonably nice Cannondale road bike that gets me around pretty well, although I also have a mountain bike. Now biking is my primary hobby, along with cross-country skiing in the winter, when we have one." - Mark -

Club Volunteering

Along with being the club Ride Director, Mark has been instrumental at the Seattle Bicycle Club in encouraging more interaction with other area clubs. He has a nice relaxed style about him that serves both himself and the club well. While Mark usually leads moderate rides, he is a strong cyclist and doesn't flinch at even the toughest challenges as was evident last year when he rode Pedal the Peaks in Wyoming and Montana.
