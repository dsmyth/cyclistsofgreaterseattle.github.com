Tour Schedule for 2009

June 12, 13 and 14 Columbia Gorge

This tour will include the Gorge Ride, sponsored by the Friends of the Historic Columbia Gorge Highway.  We will stay at the Columbia River Gorge Hostel in the Historic Bingen Schoolhouse.

 

July 24, 25 and 26 Beautiful Birch Bay

Over the course of 3 days we will cycle to Lummi Island, Lynden and Peace Arch Park.

 

August 28, 29 and 30 The San Juan Islands Tour

We will stay in Friday Harbor at Wayfarers Inn and cycle San Juan Island, Orcas and Lopez.

April 25 and 26 - A Sunny Yakima Weekend

Come and ride in sunny Yakima where there is very little traffic and the apples trees will be blooming. You might want to plan to do a little wine tasting while you are there since it will be the Spring Barrel Tasting Event Weekend and all the wineries will be open and having special tastings and food.

 

Saturday: Yakima Ridges Ride 

Ride Start: Start time between 7am and 9:30 (Moderate 50 mile riders meet at 8:30)  

Meet At: Fred Meyer by Chesterly Park

Ride Distance: Choice of 25, 50, 70, 100 miles

Registration Fee: $40.00 in advance.
Details: Winding over the ridges of the upper Yakima Valley, each route features rewarding landmarks of the area.  The ride is fully supported and includes break stops, lunch along the route and a celebration with food back at the finish line. 

Details will be posted soon on Apple Valley Kiwanis Bicycle Rides web site

www.desertvalley.com/rides

 

Sunday: Yakima to Tieton Loop

Ride Start: Motel 6

Meet At: 9:00 am leave 9:15 am
Ride Pace: Moderate.
Ride Distance: 40 miles.
Ride Terrain: Mostly flat with one hill in the beginning, very little traffic. 

Details: We will have lunch in Tieton.

 

Lodging: This is a busy weekend in Yakima with the Spring Barrel Tasting. Motel 6 is in Yakima, Exit 31.  Rooms are approx. $52.00 single or $58.00 double when booked on their web site. Camping is available at Yakima Sportsman State Park, which is a very nice facility.

 

Use the COGS email if you need a roommate or want to car pool.

July 24, 25 and 26 Birch Bay Tour

Come spend 3 days biking the rural roads of Whatcom County. We will be staying in beautiful Birch Bay where we will ride along the bay, have Dutch pastries in Lynden and visit the Peace Arch Park. Evenings can be spent walking along the beach and enjoying the sunset. 

Friday Ride: Fairhaven to Lummi Island 
Time:  10:00 start 
Meet: Colophon Café in Fairhaven 
Distance: 40 miles 
Pace: Moderate (14-16 mph) 
Ride Terrain: Rolling with a few hills

Ride Description: We will meet at the Colophon Café in Fairhaven (S. Bellingham). We will bike along the bay and then take the ferry to Lummi Island for lunch. When we return we will have dinner at the Colophon Café before going to the Hostel. 

Directions to Colophon Café: From I-5: Take exit 250 off of the Freeway. Head west on Old Fairhaven Parkway about 1.6 mi. until you reach a stop light. Continue straight through the light, the street winds to the right becoming 10th Street - heading north. Proceed straight through the stop sign and park your car on the street. Café is located on Fairhaven Village Green. 

Saturday Ride: Birch Bay to Lynden 
Time: 9:30  
Meet: Birch Bay Hostel 
Distance: 40 miles

Pace: Moderate

Ride Terrain: Mostly flat with a few hills 
Ride Description: We will ride to the Dutch town of Lynden for lunch and spend some time walking around the town. This is a nice rural ride with very little traffic. On the return to the hostel we will stop at the grocery to purchase food for dinner at the hostel. (Also lunch for Sunday)

 
Sunday Ride: Birch Bay to Peace Arch Park

Time:  9:00 start 
Meet: Birch Bay Hostel 
Distance: 40 miles 
Pace: Moderate 
Ride Terrain: Mostly flat with a few hills

Ride Description:  We will bike along the shore of Birch Bay heading north to the Peace Arch.    While at the park we will have lunch and enjoy the flower display and sculpture exhibit. The return ride will take us on a tour of the country side with a stop in Custer for ice cream. 

Lodging: We will be staying at the Birch Bay Hostel just 3 blocks from the beach. Make your own reservations at the hostel.  They have a limited number of private rooms.  All other rooms are shared rooms that accommodate 3 people. Linen and towels are provided.  Cost is $20 for a shared room or $45 for a private room with bath. Let them know you are with COGS. Hostel phone reservations: 360-371-2180

Kitchen facilities are available if you would like to bring food.  There are grocery stores in the area. We will do a potluck dinner on Saturday at the hostel. 

Directions to hostel: From Seattle-take I-5 north to Exit 266, turn left, go six miles to Blaine Road. Turn right, and go north two miles. Turn left on Alderson Road (flashing yellow light), then left again into Bay Horizon Park. Hostel is fourth building on the right. 

Tour Coordinators: Mary Jo at maryjo1532@hotmail.com

Use the COGS email if you need a roommate or want to carpool.
