COGS 7 Day Cycling Tour of the Idaho Panhandle

Saturday to Saturday – August 16th to August 23rd 2014

COGS is offering a beautiful seven day bike tour in the Panhandle region of Idaho starting and finishing in Coeur d’Alene, Idaho from August 16th to 23rd, 2014. This promises to be a fun-filled and adventuresome COGS bike tour.


Recall that Carol and Ralph Nussbaum led a very successful tour from Eastern Washington into British Columbia and back this past June for our club. COGS has again requested Ralph and Carol – long time COGS members, who for the past 12 years have been organizing and leading professional bike tours as volunteers around the country - to coordinate and lead the Idaho ride. They have agreed to do so "at cost". They will be our ride leaders and will make all the tour arrangements including providing cue sheets and maps for each day along with lots of ideas for interesting things to do en route.


This seven day Idaho tour is 375 miles in length and has something for everyone, tremendous variety in scenery, mountain passes, lakes and streams, plentiful opportunities for sighting wildlife and two beautiful paved trail systems. The route starts and ends in Coeur d'Alene and travels north on quiet back roads along Hayden Lake (perhaps named after a long lost relative of our Mike Hayden?) to Sandpoint, ID. Route follows Lake Pend Oreille and Clark Fork River into Montana and then heads southwest over Thompson and Dobson Passes to the historic mining town of Wallace, ID where we will spend a rest day. For an additional $75, many folks will want to rent mountain bikes and ride the Trail of the Hiawatha along the old Great Northern Railroad bed. We then follow the spectacular Trail of the Coeur d’Alenes to Harrison, ID. The following day we enter Washington and ride north to Spokane, where we pick up the Centennial Trail back to Coeur d’Alene.


The ride is intended for riders with moderately high biking skill levels or better. Everyone can ride at their own comfortable pace. Be aware though that there are two 80 mile days and we will be climbing 3,000' – 5,000' on three days. There will be a day off in Wallace. The trip will be supported with a dedicated SAG van and a support truck to haul all of our stuff. If you’re having an off day or have a mechanical you can always catch a ride in the SAG van. The cost of the tour includes the SAG truck and van as well as motel lodging, snacks and lunch along the way. No camping needed on this trip! Breakfast and dinner are on our own with several motels throwing in breakfast at no additional cost.


The ride is open to COGS members and COGS friends. The ride will be limited to the first 30 riders that sign up. The plan is to fill the tour with COGS riders and their friends. However, if the tour is not full (30 paid up riders) as of January 15th the tour will be opened to non - COGS riders to fill up the tour to ensure all the fixed costs are covered.


The cost of the trip is $825 per participant (plus an additional $75 if you want to rent the mountain bike and ride the Trail of the Hiawatha. This includes the cost of the per diem for the ride leaders and SAG drivers, motels, vehicle support, snacks/lunch and other miscellaneous costs associated with organizing and fielding the trip. 25% ($225) of the cost for each rider will be collected at time of registration and the remainder is due 90 days prior to the event ($600 due no later than May 15th). Go here to see the COGS Refund/Transfer Policy.


All participants will need to be or become COGS members so that COGS insurance covers everyone on the ride. If you are not a COGS member you must submit an application to join COGS with the annual club dues of $16 when you submit your application and deposit for the ride.

How do I sign up?

Carefully read all about the tour on the 2014 Idaho Tour Full Description, and check out the complete map of the tour route. Note the refund and transfer policies as well as the single supplement as explained on the attached registration form.

Signing up for the tour is a two step process:

  1. Fill out the  Tour Registration Form. Complete the form on your computer, print it out, sign the waiver on page 2 and mail the completed registration to the address found on the form.
  2. Use PayPal to pay your deposit. Click on the "Add to Cart" button below to make your initial $225 deposit. Once that is in your PayPal Shopping Cart you can "Continue Shopping" to add a single supplement ($475 if you want a private room). We are hoping that most folks will also join us on the Trail of Hiawatha in which case you want to add "Mountain Bike Rental" to your Shopping Cart.
  3. Ralph or Carol will send a confirmation email when they receive your registration and your deposit. If you prefer, you can mail a check to Ralph & Carol at the address on the registration form. If your COGS Membership has lapsed please make sure you renew your membership on the website Membership Registration Page. Do not send your membership dues money to Ralph & Carol! Note that the tour registration is not complete until you have paid the deposit and sent the registration form to Ralph & Carol. Remember your final payment of $600 is due no later than May 15th. Also, after January 15th, the tour registration will open up to those who are not COGS members.
