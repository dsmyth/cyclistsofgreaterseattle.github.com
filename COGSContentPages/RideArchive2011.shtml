Thoughts for 2012 from COGS' Rides Coordinator, Tom Tanner

Hello COGS Riders:

As the COGS Ride Coordinator for the remainder of 2011 and for 2012, I would like to share my ride program vision with everyone.  I would also like your input and support to make 2012 a great rides program. I reviewed this information with COGS Board of Directors and received their concurrence.

Here is the COGS Rides Program (see the COGS website for more details):

Wednesday Night Rides

COGS expanded the Wednesday night rides to having two ride groups. One group is a strenuous paced ride group. The other group is a moderate to high moderate paced group. The turnout for these two groups continues to be good. These rides operate year round.

Winter Saturday Rides

At the initiation of Richard Petters COGS is offering rides on several Saturdays a month but only when the weather is agreeable. The rides are 30 to 50 miles in length and led at the moderate to brisk pace depending upon the ride leader and the participating riders. These rides will continue until about the March time frame when we will kick in the Spring Ride Training Series discussed below.

Spring Ride Training Series

The intent is to re-initiate a robust training series for 2012 with a multi month schedule of rides starting in March and going into the summer. We plan to offer ride opportunities on most weekends during this time period at the moderate to brisk pace. I am seeking volunteers to help plan our multi month training series. This part of the program will also require a goodly number of ride leader volunteers.

 COGS participation in the Bicycle Tour of Colorado

This is an opportunity for COGS members to participate in the annual Bicycle Tour of Colorado (BTC). BTC is a week long ride through the Colorado Rocky Mountains. It is a challenging and beautiful week long ride suitable for riders with moderate or better biking skills. The event is June 17th to 23rd. We have over 25 COGS members and friends of COGS committed to doing the ride together in 2012. It is not too late to join in on this adventure.

 Social and low moderate rides

The above rides are suitable for moderate and/or higher skilled riders. The COGS Board of Directors would like to explore establishing rides for social/low moderate COGS riders. For this to work we would need a volunteer to be the social/low moderate ride coordinator, riders to volunteer occasionally to lead rides at the social/low moderate pace and COGS members willing to come out and participate on social/low moderate led rides.

 SURVEY: To help gauge the interests of the COGS membership in establishing a social/low moderate program COGS members will be ask to participate in a survey. We will be sending out by separate email a survey to gather member input and help the COGS Board of Directors gauge the interests of having a social/low moderate ride group. 

 Other COGS-sponsored rides

 There are several other ways in which COGS members can participate in riding that are not part of the above Ride Program including:

·         Summer Weekend Rides: Mary Jo Gerst organizes and leads a fantastic set of weekend rides to various and interesting locations. This is a great way for social/low moderate riders as well as other skilled riders to enjoy a wonderful, adventure filled weekend with COGS friends riding bikes.

 ·   RAPSody: Each year COGS helps sponsor a world class two day Ride Around Puget Sound known as    RAPSody  on the last weekend of August. COGS riders have habitually ridden this great ride.

 ·         COGS members participate in other rides offered by other cycling organizations. Flying Wheels, STP, RSVP, RAMROD and others are often populated by COGS riders who have banded together to ride these events. COGS riders are encouraged to participate in these events and wear the COGS “colors” – those great looking COGS jerseys and vests created by our very own Phyllis Counts.

 ·         Any COGS riders can simply post a ride inviting others to join in on their ride. Some of these rides have been our most successful rides over the years.

 The above summarizes the COGS Ride Program and gives you insight into what you can expect in terms of ride opportunities over the upcoming year. We welcome any and all constructive feedback on how to strengthen our COGS ride program.

  I look forward to your help in implementing the COGS Ride Program by participating in the rides, volunteering to be a ride leader, volunteering to help build the Spring Ride Training Series and/or volunteering to become a ride coordinator for one of the above programs.  In particular, we will need a social/low moderate ride coordinator to establish and launch a social/moderate ride program.

 Thanks, and enjoy your rides!

Details: The Snoqualmie Tunnel was closed for a couple years but reopened
this last July. It closes for the winter on Nov. 1, so this is the
next to last weekend to ride it this year. The route includes 2.0 miles

2.2 miles long and very dark, really good lights are required unless you
are a bat. Thanks to Sara Matoi for the original "Over and Under" name.
