Saturday, July 21  Fat Tire Ride, COGS Style

Over and Under Snoqualmie w/ Mark Petersen



For something a little bit different, bring a good light, as well as a hybrid or
mountain bike; i.e. anything with fatter tires. 

Meet:  9 am at Tibbets Valley Park in Issaquah.  I-90 eastbound, take the
exit 15,
SR900, turn left on Newport Way, then the first right turn into Tibbets
Valley Park.
There are restrooms there.  Then we'll drive to Exit 38 (aka Ollalie State
Park) on I-90
to the parking lot named "Twin Falls Natural Area, Discover Pass required.
Alternatively
meet at the parking lot.

The ride follows the frontage road to get on I-90 at mp 40.  After only 2
miles on I-90,
the route turns off at exit 42 and follows Tinkham Rd (gravel), crosses I-90
at exit
47, and follows Denny Creek Rd (paved) to the top of the pass.  Next, follow
the
frontage road past the ski areas to Hyak, get on the John Wayne trail
(gravel), go
through the Snoqualmie Tunnel (2 miles long and very dark) and follow the
trail back to
Ollalie State Park, about 13 miles.

The total distance is around 38 miles, most of which is unpaved, and a
really good
light is necessary for the tunnel.  Red Mountain Coffee at the top of the
pass has
good pizza, assuming that they are open. 

The weather forecast is promising, but heavy rain would cancel the ride.

 Sunday, June 10  Hurricane Ridge! (COGS and West Sound C.C.)

RIDE NAME: Hurricane Ridge Ride jointly sponsored by Cyclist of Greater Seattle (COGS) andWest Sound Cycling Club (WSCC).
RIDEDESCRIPTION:The ride length is ~80 miles with roughly 6200 feet of climbing. The ridestarts in Sequim with a warm up ride to the Olympic National Park entrance forHurricane Ridge. After a brief regroup, we will climb the ~17 miles up to theRidge and enjoy lunch at the top.
The descent down is thrilling and after we departthe Park we will catch the Olympic Discovery Trail for the trip back to Sequimor we may simply just take the road back (a little shorter and less hilly)depending on the group’s desires.
This is a challenging but stunningly beautiful ridedesigned for those seeking to have fun, stay more or less together and providemutual support. Cheers and good smiles always welcomed. 
STARTTIME/LOCATION: June 10, 2012 –Sunday starting about 9 AM at the SequimHigh School parking lot (601 N. Sequin Ave,Sequim). Seattle riders take the 7:10 AM Edmonds Ferry to Kinston!!After getting off the ferry, drive over to Sequim’s High School parkinglot (601N. Sequim Avenue,Sequim). We will plan to leave shortly after all the cars carrying ridersarrive from the Edmond’sferry at about 9 AM.
CARPOOLING: Car pooling is stronglyencouraged! If you don’t have a car pool, please consider sending aCOGS/WSCC email seeking a ride or volunteering to drive. 
 OPTIONALSTART LOCATION/TIME: Forthose who wish to skip the warm up ride, meet at the VisitorCenter in Port Angeles. The riders from Sequim willarrive there shortly after 10 AM.
 PACE:Moderate to high moderate – let’s have a fun pace set by the ridingskills of the participants. The regrouping strategy and cue sheets for thisride facilitate riding skills from moderate to brisk. However, one should beable to sustain a long 5000+ foot climb to successful enjoy this ride. Theclimb up Hurricane Ride will be at each rider’s individual pace. Some riders will more quickly ascend; others will just take their time andeventually crest the top.
REGROUPINGPLAN: We will ride about 20miles from Sequim to the Hurricane Visitors Station at the foot of the climb.There we will regroup including a potty break and fill our water bottles. Atthe top of Hurricane Ridge we will regroup for lunch and to share stories ofour climb. After lunch we will descend back to the Visitors Centerand regroup again before heading back to Sequim via the Olympic Discovery Trailor retrace our route along the road.  
CUE SHEETS:Yes, down load if you would like one.
EQUIPMENT:Bring two water bottles. You will need both. Bring energy food to fuel yourselfduring the climb. Also bring rain and cold weather riding gear. The weather canchange quickly up on the Ridge, i.e. turn cold and wet quickly, and the descentrequires warm gear. Remember you are riding in a rain forest…don’tget caught unprepared. Also bring the typical pump, spare tubes and otherbiking gear to make yourself self sufficient. Money will be needed to pay foryour Park entrance fee and for lunch at the top. This is an unsupported ride
RAIN POLICY:Heavy rain cancels. If in doubt call one of the ride leaders.
RIDELEADERS: This ride is sponsored byCOGS and WSCC. COGS ride leader is Tom Tanner at Home 206/298.0225 Cell206/384.9400 and tjtanner@comcast.net. WSCC ride leader is Lee Derror at360/271.4838 and lderror2@yahoo.com. Please feel free to contact your rideleaders about the ride if you have any questions.___

Join Tom Tanner and other COGS riders in the event. Many of us have done this early season ride several times– it is a beautiful ride through the back roads of Snohomish County.Below are the details.

RIDE/COST: Detailsabout 2012 McClinchy Mile Ride in Arlingtonare available at this website: http://www.bikesclub.org/McClinchyRegister

Cost for day of registration is $25. It is $20 if you mailin your registration by 3/14/2012. Price includes food stops, map, andon-course support vehicle with $1 going to Bicycle Alliance of Washington. Onecan register on line or pay to register the day of the ride- they take cash orchecks only.

RIDE LEADER: TomTanner. Home Telephone- 206/298.0225 or cell number – 206/384.9400

ROUTE, DISTANCE AND PACE: Plan to do the 47 mile route at a moderate to high moderate pace. One can ride less (34mile loop) or more (58 mile loop) or combine several loops to achieve 100 milecentury ride. Participants can elect to ride whatever pace suits them. We suggest COG riders ride more or less together or at least meet up at the rest stops to munch food, sip fluids, compare notes and kibitz about who knows what.

TIME: The event is March17th, Saturday. Plan to start riding about 9:30 AM. We suggest COGS riders arrive at the starting area about 9 AM or a little later to have time to park, procure your registration, use bathrooms and generally get ready for a 9:30 AM departure.
