Between The NOX - Tuesday Evening Series.

Ride Days: Every Tuesday Evening starting March 31 unless otherwise noted.
Ride Start: Jack Block Park, West Seattle. On Harbor Avenue, South of Salty's Restaurant.
Meet At: 5:45 pm, start 6:00pm sharp.
Ride Pace: Brisk 16 - 18 MPH, sometimes Strenous 18 - 20 MPH.
Ride Distance: 25 - 40 Miles.
Ride Terrain: Mixed / Hilly
Ride Lead:  Dennis Grace
Contact Info: 206-937-8210 home
E-mail: coupdegrace@hotmail.com
Rain Policy: Showers/rain cancels.
Ride Description:  A training ride from West Seattle, scheduled for evenings during the spring, summer and fall, 'between the vernal and autumnal equinox'. There are no cue sheets, we regroup often at major turns in the routes, so not to leave someone behind lost in a neighborhood that they maybe unfamiliar with. We do not ride pacelines and we are not a stay together ride either, however we all do ride at a similar pace and therefore we do stay together most of the time. Most of the riders who ride know the routes and are welcome to go off the front and have as much fun as they care to.  Be prepared to handle your minor mechanical problems, flat tires etc. Lights are recommended but not required.  We will try to  finish before dark. Bring sufficient drinks and extra food for a two to three hour ride.

Saturday, August 8, 2009   Mount Baker

CANCELLED DUE TO EXPECTED INCLEMENT WEATHER

Training Series Ride

Meet at: in the little community of Glacier which is located on SR- 542 (Mt Baker Highway). Suggest one park near the store in some out of the way spot.

Meet Time:   Meet at 9:30 AM with a departure shortly thereafter. If you are late we will meet you at the top at Artist Point. It is about 120+ mile drive that takes about 2 ½ hours to drive from Seattle so plan accordingly.

Pace: Climb at your own pace

Regroup: At the top at Artist's Point, which is about 35 miles from Glacier

Distance: 70 miles;   about 5000 feet of climbing

Cue Sheet: None needed.   Just follow Mt Baker Road (SR-542) up the mountain until you reach Artist Point

Terrain:Very Hilly

Rain Policy: Rain, other than sprinkles, cancels

Ride Leader: Tom Tanner (Cell 206/384.9400) and Ron Tazuma (Cell: 425/830.6629)

Description: The ride this weekend is up beautiful Mount Baker. We depart from the little community of Glacier and climb about 30 miles and about 5000 feet. There are three really great parts to the ride. The first is the climb up Mt Baker Highway…one of the best mountain climbs in the Pacific Northwest- good road and usually not much traffic. The scenery is to die for!! The second best part is arriving at Artist Point at the end of the road. The vistas are nothing short of magnificent!! The third best part is the down hill descent—WOW!!.

Car pooling is strongly encouraged. If you need help arranging use the COGS email to solicit help from others. If you can give a ride please let it be known.

Saturday August 22 and Sunday, August 23, 2009 RAPSODY - Ride Around Puget Sound

The RIDE: This is the premier ride around the Puget Sound including crossing the new Tacoma Narrows Bridge and traveling through five different counties on country roads and bikeways.  It also includes a night of camaraderie, food, drink, live music and general merriment in downtown metropolitan Shelton. The route is about 170 miles with elevation to challenge us all. We will do the ride in two days and camp free at the Shelton High School. The first day we will bike 89 miles and climb 5800 feet and on the second day we will bike 81 miles and climb 3800 feet. Check out the route at http://www.rapsodybikeride. com/route.html

When:  August 22nd and 23rd which is a Saturday/Sunday weekend. Plan is to meet at 7:30 AM and depart shortly thereafter. Plan is to ride more or less together as a group of friends regrouping at all the delicious food and watering stops planned for our enjoyment along the way. Of course, you can also start out with the group and “hammer” ahead if you so desire. Another possibility is that smaller groups of like minded riders will elect to ride together. Whatever, we will all enjoy each others company in Shelton Saturday evening.

Logistics: There is a bit of logistics associated with doing this ride. First you must register for the ride. You can do that at http://www.rapsodybikeride. com/register.html Register prior to July 18th and the cost is $80. Register after July 18th the cost is $90. Members of Bicycle Alliance save an additional $10. 

You will need to bring your camping gear and drop at the starting point.  Luggage transportation to Shelton and back to start are included in the registration fee.  REad the registration info and ensure you have all the logistic bases covered.  Free parking is provided for our cars.

Why: There are two reasons. The first is the ride is awesome. It is a must do ride. It is beautiful ride in parts of Washington many of us rarely have the opportunity to experience. Everyone that has ever done the ride says “great ride” , “terrific ride”, “I would do it again”! The second reason is all the proceeds from the ride go to the Bike Alliance of Washington. The ride is sponsored and hosted by 5 small bike clubs, COGS being one of them. Our Bob Nyberg and others have put countless hours into preparing this ride for us and to help raise funds to support the Bike Alliance of Washington.

Ride Leader: Tom Tanner or self-lead.  The ride is supported.
